# SI.4 Biomass stoichiometry

Reaction stoichiometry for algae growth on ammonium and nitrate is reported below (Equation SI4.1 and SI4.2).

Stoichiometric coefficients were computed in order to respect elemental and charge balance.

---

$$
100\, \text{CO}_2 + 11\, \text{NH}_3 + \text{H}_3\text{PO}_4 + 73.5\, \text{H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow \text{C}_{100}\text{H}_{183}\text{O}_{48}\text{N}_{11}\text{P} + 114.75\, \text{O}_2
$$

(SI4.1)

$$
100\, \text{CO}_2 + 11\, \text{HNO}_3 + \text{H}_3\text{PO}_4 + 84.5\, \text{H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow \text{C}_{100}\text{H}_{183}\text{O}_{48}\text{N}_{11}\text{P} + 136.75\, \text{O}_2
$$

(SI4.2)

---

The Chemical Oxygen Demand, (COD), associated to the algal biomass was estimated:

$$
114.75 \left[\frac{\text{mol}_{\text{O}_2}}{\text{mol}_{\text{BM}}}\right] \cdot 32 \left[\frac{\text{g}_{\text{O}_2}}{\text{mol}_{\text{O}_2}}\right] = 3672 \left[\frac{\text{g}_{\text{COD}}}{\text{mol}_{\text{BM}}}\right]
$$

(SI4.3)

$$
\frac{3672 \left[\frac{\text{g}_{\text{COD}}}{\text{mol}_{\text{BM}}}\right]}{2336 \left[\frac{\text{g}_{\text{BM}}}{\text{mol}_{\text{BM}}}\right]} = 1.57 \left[\frac{\text{g}_{\text{COD}}}{\text{g}_{\text{BM}}}\right]
$$

(SI4.4)

---

The stoichiometric equation also allows for the assessment of the mass of each element required for biomass synthesis (i.e. $i_{X, \text{BM}} [gX gCOD^{-1}]$, with X: C, N, O, P, H). As an example, in Equation SI4.5 the estimation of $i_{N, \text{BM}}^{\text{ALG}}$ related to reaction SI4.1 is reported. The same calculation was performed for $i_{P, \text{BM}}^{\text{ALG}}$, $i_{O, \text{BM}}^{\text{ALG}}$, $i_{H, \text{BM}}^{\text{ALG}}$.

$$
i_{N, \text{BM}}^{\text{ALG}} = \frac{11 \left[\frac{\text{mol}_{\text{N}}}{\text{mol}_{\text{BM}}}\right] \cdot 14 \left[\frac{\text{g}_{\text{N}}}{\text{mol}_{\text{N}}}\right]}{3672 \left[\frac{\text{g}_{\text{COD}}}{\text{mol}_{\text{BM}}}\right]} = 0.042 \left[\frac{\text{g}_{\text{N}}}{\text{g}_{\text{COD}}}\right]
$$

(SI4.5)

---

A similar approach was applied for the growth of the bacterial biomass. The biomass elementary composition assumed for both heterotrophic and nitrifying bacteria was taken from Metcalf and Eddy (2014), i.e. $\text{C}_{60}\text{H}_{87}\text{O}_{23}\text{N}_{12}\text{P}$.

$$
\text{C}_{60}\text{H}_{87}\text{O}_{23}\text{N}_{12}\text{P} + 62.25\, \text{O}_2 \rightarrow 60\, \text{CO}_2 + 12\, \text{NH}_3 + \text{H}_3\text{PO}_4 + 23.5\, \text{H}_2\text{O}
$$

(SI4.6)

$$
62.25 \left[\frac{\text{mol}_{\text{O}_2}}{\text{mol}_{\text{BM}}}\right] \cdot 32 \left[\frac{\text{g}_{\text{O}_2}}{\text{mol}_{\text{O}_2}}\right] = 1992 \left[\frac{\text{g}_{\text{COD}}}{\text{mol}_{\text{BM}}}\right]
$$

(SI4.7)

$$
\frac{1992 \left[\frac{\text{g}_{\text{COD}}}{\text{mol}_{\text{BM}}}\right]}{1373 \left[\frac{\text{g}_{\text{BM}}}{\text{mol}_{\text{BM}}}\right]} = 1.45 \left[\frac{\text{g}_{\text{COD}}}{\text{g}_{\text{BM}}}\right]
$$

(SI4.8)

---

**NOTES:** 
* It is worth emphasising that the ALBA model accounts for P assimilation while existing models do rarely consider P in the biomass raw formula.

* Stoichiometric coefficients for hydrolysis and ammonification were computed by imposing the element continuity, as shown earlier for algal growth.
